Isn't it just run?

Running is an incredibly beneficial physical activity for your health, providing countless benefits for the body and mind. However, it is vital to approach this practice with caution and consider a gradual, well-planned process. Running intensely and quickly right away can increase your risk of injury and exhaustion, compromising your long-term benefits.

The body needs time to adapt to the repetitive impact of running, strengthen muscles and adjust to increased cardiovascular demands. For this reason, it is crucial to take a gradual approach at the beginning of training. Starting with shorter sessions at a moderate pace allows the body to get used to it progressively, reducing the risk of injury.

Furthermore, before starting any running program, it is highly recommended to consult a doctor or healthcare professional. They can assess your physical condition, identify any restrictions or medical concerns, and offer personalized guidance to ensure running is safe and effective for you.

A personal trainer can also be a valuable source of guidance, designing a training program tailored to your individual needs. This professional can help create realistic goals, adjust training volume and intensity, and provide guidance on technique and injury prevention.

Remember, running is a journey, not a race against time. Consistency and patience are essential to building a solid and sustainable foundation. Enjoy the process, listen to your body, and gradually adjust the intensity as you go. By taking a balanced approach and respecting your body's limits, you maximize the benefits of running while minimizing the associated risks.

Additionally, you need to pay attention to your diet, sleep, and strengthening and stretching exercises.
