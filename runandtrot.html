What is the difference beetween run and jog?

There is a difference in the way you run, and this can vary depending on your speed, stride length and how tired you are during the run. That's why it's important to differentiate between walking, jogging and actually running.

Jogging is a versatile physical activity that can be enjoyed by people of all ages and fitness levels. The key to a successful jogging experience is to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity. Finding the right pace is crucial, where you can comfortably hold a conversation while working out. As you build endurance, you can pick up the pace and explore new levels of spee. Jogging offers numerous health benefits, including improved cardiovascular fitness, muscle strengthening, and an overall sense of well-being, making it a popular choice for those seeking an effective and accessible exercise, eventhough it's slowly.

Running is a more intense form of aerobic exercise compared to jogging. While jogging is characterized by a moderate pace, running involves a more vigorous effort and greater speed. The typical speed of running varies from person to person, but it is generally considered an activity where you reach a pace where you can no longer carry on a comfortable conversation. For many runners, this occurs at intensities that reach 70% or more of maximum heart rate. Running is an excellent way to improve endurance, burn calories and challenge your physical limits, although it requires more rigorous physical and technical preparation than jogging. Both activities offer health benefits, but running is often chosen by those seeking more intense training.
